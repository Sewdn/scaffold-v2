# RecipeVault — Project Briefing (Problem Space)

## Business Context

A food blogger and home cook wants to build a personal recipe management system that can grow into a community platform. She has hundreds of recipes in notebooks, Word docs, and bookmarks. Finding a specific recipe or planning a week of meals is tedious. She also wants to share recipes with friends and eventually allow others to contribute and organize their own collections.

The goal is to start with a personal tool, then open it to a small community before considering a broader public launch.

## Current Challenges

1. **Fragmentation**: Recipes live in multiple formats and locations. No single searchable index.
2. **Discovery**: Hard to find "that pasta recipe from last summer" or "something quick with chicken."
3. **Meal Planning**: No way to drag recipes into a weekly plan or generate shopping lists.
4. **Sharing**: Sharing means copying text or sending links; no structured way to share a collection.
5. **Scaling**: As the community grows, moderation and attribution become important.
6. **Mobile Use**: Kitchen use is often tablet or phone; the system must work well on smaller screens.

## Vision for Solution

RecipeVault will be a recipe management and sharing platform that:

- Stores recipes with structured data (ingredients, steps, servings, tags, source)
- Supports search and filtering (by ingredient, tag, time, difficulty)
- Allows users to organize recipes into collections (e.g. "Weeknight Dinners", "Holiday Favorites")
- Enables meal planning (assign recipes to days; optional shopping list) in Phase 2
- Supports sharing: public recipes, shared collections, optional attribution
- Works responsively on desktop and mobile

The platform should feel personal and inviting—like a digital recipe box.

## Business Opportunities

1. **Personal Productivity**: Faster meal planning and less time searching for recipes.
2. **Community Building**: A shared recipe space can attract subscribers and foster engagement.
3. **Monetization Path**: Premium features (meal planning, shopping lists) or sponsored content in later phases.
4. **Content Ownership**: Clear ownership and licensing support future publishing.
5. **API/Integrations**: Recipe data could power meal-planning apps or grocery delivery.

## Next Steps

Start with a personal MVP: core recipe CRUD, search, and collections. Then add sharing and meal planning in Phase 2.
