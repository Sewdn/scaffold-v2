# EventHub — Project Briefing (Problem Space)

## Business Context

A local community organization wants to help residents discover and participate in events—meetups, workshops, volunteer opportunities, and cultural activities. Currently, event information is scattered across social media, mailing lists, and flyers. People miss events they would have attended, and organizers struggle to gauge interest or manage RSVPs.

The organization is a non-profit with limited technical capacity. They need a solution that can be set up quickly, maintained by volunteers, and extended over time as the community grows.

## Current Challenges

1. **Discovery**: Residents have no single place to browse upcoming events. Information is fragmented across Facebook, newsletters, and physical posters.
2. **RSVP Management**: Organizers use ad-hoc tools (Google Forms, Doodle, spreadsheets) to track attendance. No integration with event listings.
3. **Visibility**: Small events and new organizers get little exposure. Popular events fill up without waitlists.
4. **Trust & Safety**: No way to verify organizers or flag inappropriate content. Community relies on word-of-mouth.
5. **Accessibility**: Event formats (time, location, format) are inconsistent. Hard to filter by accessibility needs or interests.
6. **Sustainability**: The organization cannot afford commercial event platforms. Any solution must be self-hosted or low-cost.

## Vision for Solution

EventHub will be a community-owned event discovery and RSVP platform that:

- Centralizes event listings with consistent metadata (date, time, location, organizer, capacity)
- Allows residents to browse, filter, and RSVP to events
- Gives organizers a simple way to create events and see who is attending
- Supports multiple event types (one-off, recurring, online, in-person)
- Can be extended with notifications, calendars, and moderation in later phases

The platform should feel lightweight and welcoming—not corporate. It should work well on mobile and desktop.

## Business Opportunities

1. **Community Engagement**: Higher participation in local activities strengthens the community.
2. **Organizer Support**: Easier event management attracts more organizers and diverse events.
3. **Partnerships**: Other organizations (libraries, schools, NGOs) could adopt or federate with EventHub.
4. **Data Insights**: Aggregated, anonymized data on event trends could inform local policy and funding.
5. **Open Source**: The platform could be shared with other communities, building reputation and contributor base.

## Next Steps

The organization has approved an MVP scope. The next phase is to define the domain, scope, and technical approach, then scaffold and implement the initial version.
